I

The Ensemble

Four musicians, one shared obsession: to let music seven centuries old sound alive in the room, not behind glass.

The Hawthorn Consort was founded to perform the repertoire of medieval and early Renaissance Europe on the instruments it was written for — plucked gittern, soft recorder, the bright wire of a Gothic harp, and the unadorned human voice.

We draw on surviving manuscripts and the latest scholarship, but our aim is never the museum. A Machaut ballade, an Iberian cantiga, an anonymous English carol — each is a living thing, shaped anew for every audience and every acoustic, from village church to festival hall.

Equally at home in concert programmes, liturgy and intimate private performance, the consort has appeared across Europe in chapels, cloisters and historic houses.
